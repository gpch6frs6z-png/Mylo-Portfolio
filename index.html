Mylo

Frame by frame,
story by story.

I started editing because I couldn't stop noticing how a single cut changes the entire feeling of a scene. What began as gaming montages turned into ads, motion graphics, and cinematic short-form, whatever the story demanded, I learned to deliver it.

I don't just cut footage. I think about what the viewer feels at every second, where their attention goes, what keeps them watching. Quality, rhythm, and on-time delivery aren't extras, they're the baseline.

How I work
01
Brief first, cuts second
I read the project brief and make a whole layout. The clearer the direction, the sharper the result will be.
02
One revision, rarely two
You won't need any more than two revisions, not only because of the efficiency, because I listen.
03
Sound is half the edit
I treat audio as seriously as the cut. Sync, transitions, and music selection are chosen intentionally, not slapped on at the end.
04
Deadline is the deadline
I've never handed in a file late and I don't intend to start. I intend to deliver as said.
05
Clean delivery, every time
Organized delivery with labelled files. You won't need to ask which file is which, I also consider conveniency.
